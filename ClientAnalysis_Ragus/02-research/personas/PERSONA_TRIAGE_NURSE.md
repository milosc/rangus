## Background

Carol has been the Head Nurse at the ER for 10 years, with 20 years of total emergency nursing experience. She's the critical link between intake and doctor evaluation - responsible for taking vitals, assigning initial ESI scores, and preparing patients for medical review. Carol is also the "workflow coordinator," ensuring that patients don't get stuck in bottlenecks between intake and triage.

She works rotating shifts, including nights, where she struggles with bright white screens that hurt her eyes after hours of staring. Carol is highly skilled at reading patients - she knows when someone's vital signs look stable on paper but their behavior or appearance suggests something more serious. She adds detailed clinical notes for doctors, documenting observations that aren't captured by blood pressure cuffs or thermometers.

Carol's biggest challenge is managing float staff during vacation coverage or sick days. When temporary nurses cover triage, they often struggle with complex systems that require extensive training. She needs tools that are intuitive enough for someone to sit down and use effectively within 30 seconds - no manuals, no training sessions.

## Design Implications

### Mobile UI Requirements
- **Dark mode toggle** - Essential for night shift staff to reduce eye strain
- **Touch targets MUST be 44x44px minimum** - Accommodate gloved hands
- **High-contrast color schemes** - Both light and dark modes must have strong contrast
- **Large, clear fonts** - Readable without squinting during 12-hour shifts
- **Single-screen triage form** - No multi-page wizards

### Workflow Requirements
- **Streamlined vitals entry** - Quick numeric keypad for BP, HR, O2, Temp with large buttons
- **ESI level selection** - Visual scale (1-5) with color coding and descriptions
- **Clinical notes templates** - Preset phrases for common observations ("Patient grimacing", "Appears anxious", "Profuse sweating")
- **Auto-save on field change** - No manual "Save" button required
- **Visual workflow state indicators** - Clear status of where patient is in triage process

### Training Requirements
- **Zero formal training** - System must be self-explanatory within 30 seconds
- **Contextual tooltips** - Inline help for ESI guidelines without leaving screen
- **Consistent layout** - Same button positions across all triage screens
- **Visual cues for required fields** - Color coding or icons to show what's needed
